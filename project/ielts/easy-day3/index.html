雅思语料库 · 简单版 - Day 3

简单版 — C20 Test 3 (Museums / Satisfied with work / Job satisfaction)

一、Part 1: Museums

Q1: Did you enjoy museums as a child?

“I didn’t go to museums much on my own as a kid. The times I went were with my parents or on school trips, and I really loved it. Seeing real dinosaur bones in person felt way more exciting than reading about them.”

Q2: Are there interesting museums near where you live?

“Yes, Shanghai has tons of museums. My favourite is the Natural History Museum. You can get really close to animal skeletons. Standing under those huge prehistoric creatures — it helps me disconnect from daily stress.”

Q3: Is it better to go to museums alone or with friends?

“I prefer going solo. With friends you’re always checking if they’re bored. Alone, I can wander around freely and really take it all in. It’s almost like meditation.”

Q4: Is it important to visit museums when you travel?

“Absolutely. Museums help you understand the history and culture of a place. Plus, during holidays everyone’s fighting to get tickets — they’re always sold out.”

二、Part 2: Describe a piece of work you felt satisfied with

“A piece of work I’m proud of is an IELTS speaking practice app I made. You paste in any text and a natural voice reads it back to you.

I built it because I was prepping for IELTS and got tired of just reading to myself. I wanted to hear my material spoken out loud. AI did the heavy lifting on the voice, but my friends helped a lot too. I roped in about ten people to test it. Their feedback was really positive.

What made me satisfied was that this was my first-ever product built with AI. It felt like I’d actually crossed a personal milestone.”

三、Part 3: Job satisfaction

Q1: What makes people dissatisfied in life?

“Mostly money and time. When you’re a student, you’re flat broke but have all the time in the world. When you start working, you have money but no time. It’s like you’re always trading time for stuff.”

Q2: Is having ambitions always positive?

“Yes, mostly. Ambitions bring passion and energy to your life. But you don’t need to be grinding away every day. Sometimes you just need to rest, and ambitions act like a reset button to pull you back up.”

Q3: What makes a satisfying life?

“Health is number one. Then I’d say be kind to yourself, take life day by day, and have loved ones around you. A bit of money doesn’t hurt either.”

Q4: What makes a job satisfying — salary or colleagues?

“The job itself matters most — it needs to be something you care about. Good colleagues and a nice atmosphere matter too. When you’re doing what you love with people you click with, the money usually follows.”

Q5: Do people need to change jobs to stay satisfied?

“It depends. If you’re always after a bigger salary, maybe yes. But if you care about the work atmosphere and work-life balance, I’d say find a place that feels right and settle in.”

Q6: Can people find satisfaction in any type of work?

“I think yes. If you’re truly passionate, you’ll find meaning. It could be about helping society or satisfying your own interests. Every type of work has its own unique value.”

  • flat broke — 身无分文
  • all the time in the world — 大把时间
