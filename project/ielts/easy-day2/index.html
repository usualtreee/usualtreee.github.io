雅思语料库 · 简单版 - Day 2

简单版 — C20 Test 2 (Fruit / Changed a plan / Career)

一、Part 1: Fruit

Q1: What’s your favourite fruit?

“Definitely mango. It’s sweet but not too sweet. I also have a childhood memory of a mango tree near my house. My mum would pick them for me. It makes me feel nostalgic.”

Q2: Are there any fruits you don’t like?

“Not really. But if I had to pick, I’d say apples. They’re healthy, but they’re just so common. Compared to mangoes, they’re not very juicy — a bit bland.”

Q3: Do you like cooked food with fruit in it?

“Not really. Hot fruit in a savoury dish feels weird — it gets all mushy and overly sweet. The only one I can accept is pineapple, like in sweet-and-sour pork.”

Q4: Where’s the best place to buy fruit?

“The shop on campus is overpriced, so I buy from small stalls outside the gate. They’re much cheaper. Sometimes I go to Hema, a fresh grocery store — better quality but the street stalls are unbeatable on price.”

二、Part 2: Describe a time you changed a plan

“I’d like to talk about the time I changed my plan right after my postgraduate interview. My original plan was to fly to Yinchuan to see friends and unwind. But then I realized my girlfriend’s graduation was coming up really soon.

So I cancelled the trip and headed straight to Fuzhou instead. I felt really happy I made it to her ceremony. It meant a lot to be there for her big day, and I was excited to share my good news in person.”

三、Part 3: Plans / Career

Q1: What plans do friends make together?

“Mostly travel plans. My friends and I are always planning short trips — just hopping on a train to nearby cities, trying restaurants, and checking out sights.”

Q2: Is it better to discuss plans with friends or family?

“Both. Family gives you emotional support and encouragement. Friends can give you different perspectives because they face similar challenges.”

Q3: Should you avoid copying friends’ career plans?

“Yes. Everyone’s situation is different. You should listen to your own instincts and make plans based on your own strengths.”

Q4: How important is it that a degree leads to a career?

“Pretty important. University education can lag behind industry — too theory-heavy. If you want to land a job, you need to pick up practical skills on your own. But the foundation you get at university is still important.”

Q5: Is work experience important before choosing a career?

“Yes. You need to get your hands dirty with real work. An internship lets you learn how a company actually works. As the saying goes, you have to do it yourself to truly understand something.”

Q6: How easy is it to change careers?

“It depends. If it’s a technical role, switching takes serious effort. But for simpler roles, it’s easier. People from more demanding roles can usually switch more smoothly.”

四、核心词汇

  • nostalgic — 怀念的
  • bland — 寡淡的
  • overpriced / unbeatable — 太贵/无可比拟
  • unwind — 放松
  • made it — 成功赶到
  • meant a lot — 意义重大
  • in person — 当面
  • hopping on a train — 跳上火车
  • lag behind — 落后
  • land a job — 找到工作
  • get your hands dirty — 亲自动手
  • takes serious effort — 需要真功夫
