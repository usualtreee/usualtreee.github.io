雅思口语语料库 - Day 3

雅思口语语料库 — C20 Test 3 Speaking（Museums / Satisfied with work / Job satisfaction）

一、Part 1: Museums

Q1: Did you enjoy going to museums when you were a child?

> “To be honest, I didn’t really go to museums on my own as a kid — mainly because I didn’t have the money or the freedom to travel around the city by myself. The only times I went were when my parents took me or when the school organized a trip. And I genuinely loved it. Seeing those real historical objects and dinosaur skeletons up close felt way more exciting than just reading about them in textbooks or seeing pictures on TV.”

Q2: Are there any interesting museums near where you live now?

> “Yeah, definitely. Shanghai has tons of interesting museums, but my favorite is the Shanghai Natural History Museum. What I love about it is that you can get really close to all these animal specimens and skeletons. Standing beneath those massive skeletons of prehistoric creatures, gazing up at them — it’s like you’re looking at traces of time itself. It really helps me disconnect from the daily chaos and just reflect on life and nature.”

Q3: Do you think it is best to go to museums by yourself or with friends?

> “Honestly, I prefer going solo. With friends, you’re constantly checking if they’re bored or rushing through stuff they don’t care about. Alone? I can wander around freely, spend forever staring at whatever catches my eye, and actually take it all in. It’s almost like meditation — just me and my thoughts.”

Q4: When you visit another city or country, do you think it’s important to go to a museum there?

> “Absolutely. I feel like museums are one of the best ways to really understand what a place is about — its history, its culture, all that. Plus, you can tell it’s a shared opinion because during holidays, everyone’s fighting to get tickets. They’re always sold out.”

二、Part 2: Describe a piece of work you felt very satisfied with

三、Part 3: Being satisfied in life / Job satisfaction

Q1: What aspects of people’s lives can they often be dissatisfied with?

> “Man, where do I even start? It’s basically money and time, right? When you’re in school, you’re flat broke but you’ve got all the time in the world. Then you start working and it’s like — cool, I’ve got a bit of cash now, but when do I actually get to enjoy it? I’m always busy. There’s this song I really like, and there’s this line that goes like: ‘People are totally fine with cutting a few seconds off their lives just to buy another expensive watch.’ Like, we’re literally trading our time for stuff. It’s kind of messed up when you think about it. So yeah, my take is: stop worrying so much, just enjoy where you are right now.”

Q2: Is having ambitions in life always a positive thing?

> “Yeah, I’d say it’s definitely a positive thing. Having ambitions just brings so much passion and energy into your daily life. But I don’t think that means you need to be grinding away every waking moment. When you’re exhausted, you should absolutely let yourself rest. That’s the beauty of having ambitions — they act like a reset button. When you’re ready, they pull you back up and give you that positive drive to keep going.”

Q3: What are the most important components of a satisfying life?

> “Honestly, I’d say health is number one — without that, nothing else really matters. Then I’d say go easy on yourself; just let things flow and take life day by day. And having loved ones around you — family, close friends — that sense of being surrounded by love is huge. Oh, and having a bit of money in your pocket? Yeah, that doesn’t hurt either.”

Q4: What makes a job more satisfying: a high salary or having good colleagues?

> “Honestly, I’d say the job itself has to be something you actually care about — that’s number one. And then, solid colleagues and a good vibe at work matter a lot too. Because when you’re surrounded by people you click with, and you’re doing something you love, you just naturally bring your A-game. And at that point, the salary kind of takes care of itself.”

Q5: Do people need to change jobs regularly to stay satisfied at work?

> “Honestly, it really depends on what ‘satisfaction’ means to you. If you’re the type who’s always chasing a bigger paycheck, then sure, changing jobs regularly is probably the way to go. But if you value things like the work atmosphere and having a decent work-life balance, I don’t see the point in constantly looking for the next thing. I’d say find a place that feels right and just settle in.”

Q6: Is it possible to find job satisfaction in all types of work?

> “Honestly, I’d say yes. If you’re truly passionate about your work, you’ll naturally find meaning in it. It could be about contributing to society or simply satisfying your own interests. Either way, I believe every type of work has its own unique value.”
